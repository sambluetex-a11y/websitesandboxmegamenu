Repair And Seam Tape

Start with the job. If the insulation facing is torn, punctured, or needs a larger patch, use repair tape. If the insulation is intact and you are joining edges or overlaps, use seam tape.

Before After

6" white repair tape shown.

Choose by problem, not by roll width

The goal is to help the customer diagnose what they are fixing first, then send them to the tape that matches the job.

  • Large rips, holes, and damaged vapor barrier areas need a wider repair surface.
  • Clean overlaps and install seams need a narrower tape that is easy to run straight.
  • BlueTex white tapes are pressure activated, so firm pressure during install matters.
Repair Tape Most Popular Use 6" tape for larger holes, torn facing, and visible repairs where more coverage helps. Use 4" tape for smaller patches, multi-use repairs, stucco prep, and masking-style jobs. 6" white tape gives larger damaged areas more coverage. 4" white tape is a handy repair width for smaller patches and multi-use jobs.
6" Repair Tape4" Multi-Use Tape
Seam Tape Use seam tape when the insulation is already in place and you need to join seams, seal overlaps, finish edges, or keep the vapor barrier continuous across a clean joint. 3" white seam tape is the main interior seam tape. 2" foil seam tape supports foil-side seams and narrower detail work.
