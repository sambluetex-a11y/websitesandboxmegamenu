The Problem

Old insulation does not fail. Its vapor barrier does.

When metal building insulation looks bad, the fiberglass core is rarely the real issue. It is the thin vinyl or paper facing that wears out. Once that facing fails, moisture gets through — and everything downhill from it gets worse.

  1. Original facing tears, separates from the fiberglass, or stains over time.
  2. Warm interior moisture passes through the failed facing.
  3. Moisture moves through the fiberglass and reaches the cold metal panels.
  4. Condensation forms on the back side of the roof or wall.
  5. Insulation gets heavier, dirtier, sags, and looks worse every year.
The Solution

Install over what you have. Lock it down with TermBars.

No tear-out No demolition No spray foam

BlueTex covers the existing insulation in place. A new bright white facing replaces the failed one, and aluminum TermBars mechanically fasten the layer at every purlin and girt — the same framing the building was built on.

  • Existing insulation stays in the cavity, hidden and intact.
  • BlueTex 2mm Pro or 6mm Supreme becomes the new interior surface.
  • Aluminum TermBars mechanically clamp the layer at the framing.
  • Seams and edges sealed with BlueTex white repair tape.

How The System Works

A four-step retrofit you can run with a small crew.

No demolition phase. Most projects move directly from access setup to a finished interior in a single pass.

1

Leave the existing insulation in place

Skip the tear-out. The old fiberglass stays in the cavity and keeps doing its R-value job.

2

Roll BlueTex across the interior

2mm Pro or 6mm Supreme rolls cover the wall or roof in straight runs against the framing.

3

Fasten with aluminum TermBars

TermBars and screws clamp BlueTex tight to every purlin and girt for a permanent mechanical hold.

4

Tape seams & finish

Seal seams and edges with BlueTex white repair tape. Walk away with a bright, clean, finished interior.
