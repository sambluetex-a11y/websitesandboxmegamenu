BlueTex Cover/Repair System Overview

Cover Your Old Insulation. No Demo. No Mess. No Contractor Required.

Old metal building insulation usually does not fail because the fiberglass disappears. It usually fails because the original vapor barrier facing gets torn, stained, loose, or worn out.

Once that facing fails:

  • Warm interior moisture gets through the old facing
  • Moisture passes through the fiberglass
  • The moisture reaches the cold metal roof or wall panels
  • Condensation forms behind the insulation
  • The insulation gets heavier, dirtier, and starts to sag

The BlueTex™ Cover/Repair System installs over the existing insulation and creates a new clean interior surface. The old insulation stays in place, and the inside of the building gets a bright white, durable vapor barrier surface.

The Problem

Why old insulation keeps getting worse

Most original metal building insulation uses a light-duty facing on the inside. Over time, that facing can tear, separate, stain, or break down from age, impact, moisture, and normal building use.

Once the facing is damaged, it no longer works well as the interior vapor barrier.

That is when the problem usually starts:

  • Warm, moist air gets past the old facing
  • The fiberglass allows that moisture to move through
  • The moisture hits cold metal behind the insulation
  • Condensation forms where you cannot easily see it
  • Wet insulation becomes heavier and starts pulling down
  • Stains, tears, loose seams, and sagging keep getting worse

This is why simply patching a few torn spots usually does not solve the whole problem. The original facing has already lost its ability to do the job.

If the existing insulation is saturated or moldy, that should be addressed before covering it. The cover system is for old, torn, stained, and deteriorated insulation that is ready for a new interior surface.

The Solution

A new clean surface over the old insulation

The BlueTex™ Cover/Repair System is designed to go directly over the existing insulation in metal buildings.

Instead of tearing everything out, BlueTex is installed across the old insulation and mechanically fastened at the purlins using aluminum TermBars. The TermBars hold the BlueTex tight to the framing and help create a clean, flat, durable finish.

The result is a new bright white interior surface that helps control warm interior moisture from getting past the BlueTex layer and reaching the cold metal behind it.

What this means for the building:

  • No tear-out
  • No demolition
  • No loose fiberglass mess
  • No spray foam fumes
  • No waiting on a full insulation removal project
  • No contractor required for many owners and maintenance teams

The 50" BlueTex material is the main product direction for this system because it is easier to handle while working from a lift and fits the way most crews actually install this type of repair.
